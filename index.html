Johann Rupert: “It is easy to give away money but more difficult to find organizations that spend the time to use the funds properly and efficiently,”

It is incredibly challenging to offer property rights to marginalized people in South Africa because the homes they live in are locked up in complex land registry procedures.

 

South African billionaire Johann Rupert and his wife Gaynor, worked very hard with local officials from Stellenbosch and the Free Market Foundation to highlight the legal status of bad ownership.

 

Kaya Mandi, Klapmuts, Kylemore, Franschhoek, and Le Roux 132 were given complete title deeds by Dr. Johann Rupert and his wife Gaynor. This was achieved by Rupert’s generosity and the help of Stellenbosch Mayor Cllr Gesie van Deventer and her team working with the Free Market Foundation (FMF) Khaya Lam (my home) on land reform at Stellenb’s historic town hall.

 

The event was also attended by Tabiso Mfeya (Head of Policy and Economic Development at Stellenbosch), Leon Louw (FMF Executive Director) and Temba Nolutshungu (FMF Cape Town President).

 

For more than 40 years, the FMF has embraced the cause of turning the different forms of colonial titles in townships into a growing, unmistakable property of existing owners. Ruperts has already funded 1,000 books in Stellenbosch and 1,000 in Graff Reinet, where the father of Dr. Rupert was raised. To date, 585 title records have been given in Stellenbosch and more will follow.

 

Johann and Gaynor Rupert, in cooperation with Khaya Lam and the Municipality, made a significant difference in the lives of ordinary South Africans and particularly the poorest people living in Stellenbosch. The common aim is through property ownership to bring about meaningful economic and social progress. As soon as the occupants enter the building, they enter the hall as tenants. Once they leave, they become homeowners in possession of a completely tradable freeholder property, taking their first step towards more real economic empowerment.

 

During his speech before the title deed ceremony, Johann Rupert said that his father was incredibly fortunate in the wealth they had gained and wanted to use it for the better.

 

“It is easy to give away money but more difficult to find organizations that spend the time to use the funds properly and efficiently,” he said and commended the FMF and Khaya Lam for their ability to do this.
